# Monetization Plan

## Principle

Monetization must not reduce recommendation trust. Paid placement cannot bypass age, schedule, weather, distance, or hard safety/fit filters.

## Stage 1 — Validate utility without payment

Measure:

- weekly repeat use;
- decision speed;
- recommendation usefulness;
- outing intent;
- data-quality complaints.

Do not add a paywall before the product reliably helps families decide.

## Stage 2 — Affiliate/referral experiment

Potential eligible categories:

- museums, zoos, aquariums, farms, tours, and ticketed attractions;
- family event tickets;
- travel bookings later.

Rules:

- disclose affiliate relationships;
- recommendation score is computed before commission consideration;
- track outbound action and aggregate conversion where permitted;
- never claim a price is final without current provider data;
- review provider and app-store requirements.

## Stage 3 — SproutScout+

Working hypothesis: parents may pay for repeated planning convenience rather than access to a directory.

Possible paid features:

- multiple child profiles;
- family sharing;
- saved recurring schedule/preferences;
- advanced one- or two-stop planning;
- automatic weather backup;
- membership optimization;
- travel/weekend planning;
- calendar integration;
- unlimited history and preference learning.

Potential initial price test, not a commitment:

- monthly range around common low-cost consumer subscriptions;
- annual plan with a discount;
- free trial only after core value is clear.

Do pricing research immediately before launch; do not hard-code old assumptions into the plan.

## Stage 4 — Sponsored local businesses

Only after meaningful local demand exists.

Requirements:

- explicit `Sponsored` label;
- fit filters still apply;
- sponsorship never changes factual confidence;
- users can hide or report irrelevant promotion;
- organic and sponsored ranking effects are logged separately;
- frequency caps prevent feed takeover.

## Stage 5 — Merchant tools

Possible later products:

- event submission;
- verified amenity/hours management;
- promotional inventory;
- aggregate demand insights;
- ticket/referral analytics.

Do not build a merchant dashboard until manual operations prove recurring supply-side demand.

## Unit economics instrumentation

Track by provider and cohort:

- API cost per recommendation session;
- cost per active family;
- affiliate revenue per outbound click and active family;
- subscription conversion/churn;
- sponsored revenue without organic retention degradation;
- support/data-maintenance cost per city.

Set quotas and alerts before scaling nationwide.
